Information

What is food waste?

Food waste refers to the losses derived from the decision to discard food that still has value and is primarily associated with the behavior of wholesalers, retailers, food service providers, and consumers

Food waste is a significant problem worldwide, not just in Mexico. Food is wasted due to several factors, with the main ones being poor food distribution and inadequate purchasing planning. In 2019, global food waste reached 931 million tons, of which 61% came from households and 26% from food services. Lack of awareness, education on this issue, and inefficient food distribution are causes of such waste in our country.

Consequences of food waste

EFood waste has social and humanitarian consequences, with the most significant being food insecurity and pollution. However, there are also numerous environmental consequences. When we waste food, we also waste the water and energy required to produce it. If the discarded food rots, it starts to produce methane, a greenhouse gas even more potent than carbon dioxide.

Food waste extends beyond Mexico and North America. In the European Union, for example, around 88 million tons of food waste are generated each year, with estimated costs of 143 billion euros. This means that one-third of the food produced for human consumption is wasted. Additionally, approximately 170 million tons of CO2 were emitted due to food waste, and 173 kg per person were wasted in the European Union.

One in nine people suffers from hunger. This tragedy primarily affects the poorest nations, leaving them highly vulnerable in terms of food. Furthermore, considering that food loss occurs throughout the entire distribution and commercialization chain, opportunities for adequate nutrition decrease even further. This problem also has an indirect consequence, as many cities and municipalities spend millions of euros managing the waste generated by this waste, funds that could be directed towards vulnerable populations. This issue becomes increasingly concerning given the projected population growth in the coming years.

Food Distribution

The distribution of food in Mexico involves a series of interconnected phases that originate in primary and agro-industrial production, followed by collection, storage, and distribution at wholesale and retail levels. This process involves intermediaries who connect producers with consumers. Intermediaries play a key role as they deploy technical, logistical, and marketing processes in various supply phases. Cities, along with transportation, are strategic loci for directing distribution flows and final demand, allowing for the complex physical and geographical circulation of products.

During the phase prior to the current open economy in Mexico, food distribution relied on a series of institutional mechanisms and was dominated by state intermediation agents. These agents, in addition to directly participating or linking in various ways to primary production, constituted the articulating axis in the collection and distribution processes of food at local and regional scales to ensure popular supply. In the wholesale distribution segment, the State constructed major commercial infrastructure for the collection and distribution of basic grains, fruits, and vegetables. In retail distribution, the State contributed to the creation or promotion of various channels such as public markets, "tianguis" (street markets), mobile markets, state-owned supermarkets, trade union markets, and other specialized establishments.

How can we take Action?

There are several actions we can take from our own homes to counteract or at least reduce food waste. Here are some examples of what can be done:

1.- Weekly Menu Planning: Preparing and planning a weekly menu is a good way to see what you need to buy and prepare the necessary food without overbuying.

2.- Make a Shopping List with Essential Items: Having a shopping list helps you know what to buy, how much it costs, and the quantity of each product you need. This makes it easier to plan your menu and ensures you get everything you need.

3.- Properly Store Food: It is important to store certain foods in places where they can be properly stored. If not, they can spoil more quickly, leading to more waste in the long run.

4.- Understand the Difference Between Expiration Date and Best Before Date: Many people confuse the expiration date with the best before date, which leads them to discard their food before it actually expires. The best before date indicates the period during which the food retains its intended quality, while the expiration date indicates the point at which the food may no longer be safely consumed.

5.- Use the Leftovers for Another Meal or Recipe: By doing this, you make the most of the food you purchased, reducing waste and saving money, which is beneficial for everyone.
